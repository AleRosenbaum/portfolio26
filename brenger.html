Challenge

In 2023, 4 of the top 10 most searched items were large items that couldn't be traded with Marktplaats's transaction tools.

Research & Insights

We combined data analysis, surveys,
and interviews to understand the real situation.

Qualitative and quantitative research confirmed the main barrier wasn't cost. It was trust. Users didn't know how items would be handled, or what recourse they'd have if something went wrong.

"I was going to ship it if I knew my sofa wouldn't get damaged. Otherwise, I'd stick to pick-up only." Buyer, user interview

We decided to build a new product iteratively, delivering value in chunks and reducing risk.

Phase 1 — In-chat promotion screen

In-chat Promotion

Started by testing desirability with In-chat Promotion

Promotion banners inside the Marktplaats chat redirected users to Brenger to finalize orders. No backend integration. Just enough to measure intent and validate demand.

  • Validated demand without engineering investment
  • Brenger started +125 orders from Marktplaats users
  • Signal strong enough to invest in real integration
Phase 2 — Marktplaats checkout + Brenger booking (2 screens)

Split Payment

Item payment at Marktplaats, shipping payment at Brenger

Item payment stayed inside Marktplaats; transport booking and payment happened on Brenger's platform. Two flows linked by a handoff. Friction, but the full transaction validated.

  • First fully transacted shipments via Brenger on Marktplaats
  • Identified two-step checkout as the main conversion blocker
  • Transport satisfaction high, product-market fit confirmed
Phase 3 — Unified checkout + scheduling (2 screens)

Full Integration

Full payment at Marktplaats, appointments via Brenger

Unified the checkout: users paid for both item and transport in a single Marktplaats flow. Appointment scheduling was handled by Brenger post-purchase. Biggest friction point removed.

  • Single payment flow, two-step checkout eliminated
  • Conversion rate improved significantly vs. Phase 02
  • Brenger positioned as quality partner, not external redirect
Phase 4 — Full standalone flow (4 screens: discovery → listing → booking → confirmation)

Standalone Flow

Users could fully trade with Brenger on a standalone solution, even after a pivot

End-to-end flow inside Marktplaats: discover, negotiate, pay, and schedule transport without leaving the platform. What started as a static toggle became a permanent product feature.

  • End-to-end trade with transport, no platform switches
  • Feature retained permanently after pilot period
  • Foundation for future logistics integrations across categories

Reflection

Defining the product in increments allowed us to:

Team learnings

  • Test adoption and reset goals before scaling
  • Iterate quickly based on real feedback, reducing risk
  • Prove value early to stakeholders, reducing politics

What I learned as a designer

  • Designing for trust in peer-to-peer transactions requires clarity at every step
  • Small design details, like highlighting "insured transport", had outsized impact on conversion
  • The iterative approach helped alignment with both users and stakeholders
